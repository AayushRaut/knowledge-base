# Module 4 References

> Curated, verifiable resources for **[Deep Learning](README.md)**. Only
> canonical sources are listed.

---

## 📘 Books

- **Deep Learning** — Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, Aaron Courville. The
  standard theoretical reference. Free: <https://www.deeplearningbook.org/>.
- **Dive into Deep Learning** — Aston Zhang, Zachary Lipton, Mu Li, Alexander
  Smola. Interactive, code-first (PyTorch/TF/JAX). Free: <https://d2l.ai/>.
- **Hands-On Machine Learning with Scikit-Learn, Keras & TensorFlow** (3rd ed.) —
  Aurélien Géron. Part II covers Keras/TF deep learning practice.

## 📑 Landmark papers (all real)

- **Generative Adversarial Networks** — Goodfellow et al., 2014.
  <https://arxiv.org/abs/1406.2661>
- **Deep Residual Learning for Image Recognition (ResNet)** — He et al., 2015.
  <https://arxiv.org/abs/1512.03385>
- **Adam: A Method for Stochastic Optimization** — Kingma & Ba, 2014.
  <https://arxiv.org/abs/1412.6980>
- **Long Short-Term Memory** — Hochreiter & Schmidhuber, 1997 (Neural Computation).

## 📄 Official Documentation

- [PyTorch docs](https://pytorch.org/docs/stable/) · [PyTorch tutorials](https://pytorch.org/tutorials/)
- [TensorFlow](https://www.tensorflow.org/) · [Keras](https://keras.io/)
- [torchvision](https://pytorch.org/vision/stable/)

## 🎓 Courses & Notes

- **Stanford CS231n** — CNNs for visual recognition; superb course notes:
  <https://cs231n.github.io/>.

## ▶️ Video

- **3Blue1Brown** — the neural-network video series (gradient descent and
  backprop, visually): <https://www.youtube.com/@3blue1brown>.
- **Andrej Karpathy** — building neural nets, backprop, and language models from
  scratch: <https://www.youtube.com/@AndrejKarpathy>.
- **StatQuest** — neural-network fundamentals step by step:
  <https://www.youtube.com/@statquest>.
