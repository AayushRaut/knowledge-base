# Module 5 References

> Curated, verifiable resources for **[Natural Language Processing](README.md)**.
> Only canonical sources are listed.

---

## 📘 Books

- **Speech and Language Processing** (3rd ed. draft) — Dan Jurafsky & James H.
  Martin. The standard NLP textbook, free online:
  <https://web.stanford.edu/~jurafsky/slp3/>.
- **Natural Language Processing with Python** — Bird, Klein & Loper (the NLTK
  book). Free: <https://www.nltk.org/book/>.

## 📑 Landmark papers (all real)

- **Word2Vec** — Mikolov et al., 2013, *Efficient Estimation of Word
  Representations in Vector Space*. <https://arxiv.org/abs/1301.3781>
- **GloVe** — Pennington, Socher & Manning, 2014. Project page:
  <https://nlp.stanford.edu/projects/glove/>
- **FastText** — Bojanowski et al., 2016, *Enriching Word Vectors with Subword
  Information*. <https://arxiv.org/abs/1607.04606>
- **Sentence-BERT** — Reimers & Gurevych, 2019. <https://arxiv.org/abs/1908.10084>
- **BLEU** — Papineni et al., 2002. · **ROUGE** — Lin, 2004.

## 📄 Official Documentation

- [spaCy](https://spacy.io/) — industrial NLP pipelines (tagging, parsing, NER).
- [NLTK](https://www.nltk.org/) — classic NLP toolkit (incl. VADER).
- [gensim](https://radimrehurek.com/gensim/) — Word2Vec/FastText training.
- [Sentence-Transformers](https://www.sbert.net/) — sentence embeddings.
- [Hugging Face docs](https://huggingface.co/docs) — tokenizers, pipelines, models.
- [scikit-learn — text features](https://scikit-learn.org/stable/)

## ▶️ Video

- **Stanford CS224N** (NLP with Deep Learning) — lectures are published on
  Stanford Online's official channels.
- **StatQuest** — embeddings and NLP fundamentals: <https://www.youtube.com/@statquest>.
